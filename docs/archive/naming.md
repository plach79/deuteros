Here are some English words starting with DEUT:

- **Deuteron** - the nucleus of a deuterium atom

- **Deuteragonist** - the second most important character in a literary work (after the protagonist)

Most of these words derive from the Greek "deuteros" meaning "second." The most commonly encountered ones would be deuterium (in chemistry/physics contexts) and Deuteronomy (in religious/biblical contexts).

## Claude

I think you mean **"deuteros"** (δεύτερος) - the Greek word meaning **"second"**.

This is the root of all those "deut-" words I listed:

- **Deuterium** = "second" hydrogen (because it has an extra neutron)
- **Deuteronomy** = "second law" (it's a repetition/restatement of the law)
- **Deuteragonist** = "second actor/character"

The Greek word "deuteros" is related to "duo" (two) and gives us this productive prefix in English for things that are second in order, secondary, or involve pairs/doubling.
